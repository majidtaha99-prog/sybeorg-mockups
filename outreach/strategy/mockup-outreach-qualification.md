# Mockup outreach qualification

Updated: 2026-06-20

## Core rule

Mockups are strongest when the prospect has no real website, a weak website, a broken/placeholder site, or a current site that our mockup clearly beats at a glance.

Do not lead with a mockup when the business already has a polished, credible site. In that case, the mockup can make SYBEORG look less serious unless the improvement is specific and obvious.

## Qualify before building or sending

Score each prospect before creating/sending:

| Signal | Mockup outreach fit |
| --- | --- |
| No website link on Google Business Profile / Maps | Excellent |
| Google/Yext/GBP-style placeholder | Excellent |
| Broken site, SSL issue, dead form, missing mobile CTA | Excellent |
| Outdated/low-trust site with weak visuals | Good |
| Good site but missing one campaign/service page | Use campaign/landing-page angle, not redesign |
| Polished site with strong brand/navigation/services | Do not send generic mockup |

## Strong-site angles

If the site is already good, switch from "we redesigned your site" to one of these:

1. Conversion audit: 3 specific fixes with screenshots.
2. Paid ads landing page for one high-intent service.
3. Seasonal campaign page with tracking and call buttons.
4. Video/commercial package that improves trust and ad creative.
5. Local SEO/service-page expansion.

## Email positioning

Bad angle:

> I made you a better website mockup.

Better angle for weak/no-site prospects:

> I noticed your Google listing/site makes it harder than it should be for mobile visitors to call. I mocked up a cleaner version so you can see the direction.

Better angle for strong-site prospects:

> Your site is already solid. I noticed a few conversion opportunities around [specific issue]. I put together a quick audit / campaign idea, not a redesign pitch.

## Key finding: the old drafts are useful but too sharp for first touch

The Gmail drafts have strong hooks, but several should be softened before sending:

1. Avoid over-claiming losses like “probably costing you real calls” unless framed as “could be.”
2. Avoid harsh phrasing like “you need to see your website right now.”
3. Avoid unverified claims unless we just re-confirmed them.
4. Keep the tone helpful/local: “I noticed this, made a quick mockup, nothing pushy.”
5. Use a light CTA first: “If the direction feels useful, I can clean it up.” instead of pushing the calendar too hard.

## Revised copy strategy

### Style rules

- Subject lines should be specific but not insulting.
- First line should prove this is not spam: mention their exact business/site observation.
- The mockup should be framed as “sample/concept,” not as something they requested.
- One soft next step in a normal human voice, not a canned micro-CTA.
- Calendar link can be secondary, not the main ask.
- Do not include botty opt-out commands.

### Strongest angles by lead

- **Five Seasons:** website bug / placeholder content. This is urgent and helpful, but use the contact form, not a cold email.
- **M&P:** official public site is active; use current domain and public email. Avoid the old Tempo Apartments claim unless manually reverified in browser.
- **Platinum:** reviews and proof are great; position a better emergency/service landing page rather than attacking AI imagery.
- **Richardson:** trustworthy, long-standing local company; respectful tone works better than “audit gotcha.”
- **8 Finger:** reputation/reviews vs unfinished web presence; good copy but verify recipient source.
- **Bunch:** since-1993 and emergency-service positioning; needs recipient discovery.
